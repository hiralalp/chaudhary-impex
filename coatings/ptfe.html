Torque & Preload Advantages

PTFE coating significantly reduces the friction coefficient, allowing for more accurate torque-tension relationships:

  • Uncoated Stainless Steel: K-factor = 0.20-0.30
  • PTFE Coated: K-factor = 0.10-0.15
  • Result: Up to 50% reduction in torque required for same preload
  • Consistency: ¬±10% variation vs ¬±30% for uncoated

This means more consistent clamping force and reduced risk of over-torquing.

Installation Guidelines

  • Surface Preparation: Clean threads, remove oil/grease
  • Inspection: Check for coating damage before assembly
  • Torque: Use lower torque values than uncoated (typically 30-50% less)
  • Lubrication: Additional lubrication not required (may affect torque)
  • Retorquing: Not typically required due to low relaxation
  • Reuse: Limited reusability - inspect coating condition
