Springs

Precision engineered springs for critical applications

Spring Products Overview

TorqBolt manufactures precision engineered springs for demanding industrial applications. Our springs are wound from high-grade wire or machined from solid stock, designed to provide exact force characteristics and exceptional cycle life in extreme environments.

Industrial Spring Manufacturing

Springs are mechanical devices that store and release energy through elastic deformation. They are designed to pull, push, wind, support, lift, or protect in various industrial applications. Our springs exhibit force characteristics that increase linearly (or non-linearly for special designs) as the spring is compressed, extended, or twisted.

Custom springs are manufactured using dedicated CNC wire winding machines that can precisely wind wire through a specific number of turns over exact lengths to produce the necessary spring rate and force constant for each unique application.

Industry-Specific Applications

Aerospace Industry

Aircraft Engine Springs: Inconel X-750 and Nimonic 90 springs for turbine components, operating at temperatures exceeding 700¬∞C. These springs maintain load characteristics in high-vibration environments and resist thermal fatigue.

Landing Gear Systems: 17-7 PH stainless steel springs provide precise force control for shock absorption during landing. High cycle fatigue resistance ensures reliability over millions of compression cycles.

Control Systems: Beryllium copper springs for non-magnetic applications in avionics and instrumentation. Lightweight titanium springs reduce overall aircraft weight while maintaining strength.

Oil & Gas Industry

Subsea Valves: Hastelloy C-276 springs resist hydrogen sulfide (H2S) and chloride stress corrosion cracking in sour gas environments. Operating pressures up to 15,000 PSI at depths exceeding 3,000 meters.

Wellhead Equipment: Inconel 718 springs for high-temperature applications in thermal recovery operations. Springs maintain preload at temperatures up to 300¬∞C in steam-assisted gravity drainage (SAGD) systems.

Pipeline Valves: 316 stainless steel springs for mainline valves and emergency shutdown systems. Corrosion resistance in wet gas and condensate service.

Nuclear Power Generation

Reactor Internals: Inconel X-750 springs designed for neutron flux environments. Springs maintain mechanical properties after years of radiation exposure in pressurized water reactor (PWR) systems.

Control Rod Drive Mechanisms: Precision springs ensure accurate positioning of neutron-absorbing control rods. Materials selected for low neutron absorption cross-section.

Safety Systems: Redundant spring systems for fail-safe operation of emergency cooling and containment systems. Seismic qualification for earthquake resistance.

Medical & Pharmaceutical

Surgical Instruments: Elgiloy (cobalt-chromium) springs for medical devices requiring biocompatibility and corrosion resistance to sterilization processes (autoclave, ethylene oxide, gamma radiation).

Drug Delivery Systems: Precision compression springs in insulin pens and auto-injectors. Tight force tolerances (¬±3%) ensure accurate dosing. 316L stainless steel provides USP Class VI biocompatibility.

Implantable Devices: Titanium and MP35N springs for pacemakers, neurostimulators, and orthopedic implants. MRI-compatible materials for diagnostic imaging safety.

Automotive Industry

Valve Train Springs: Chrome-silicon steel springs for engine valve springs, operating at 5,000+ RPM. Shot peening and preset processing increase fatigue life to 200 million cycles.

Suspension Systems: High-strength coil springs for MacPherson struts and independent suspension. Progressive rate springs provide comfort at low loads and control at high loads.

Transmission Components: Wave springs and Belleville washers for clutch pack preload. Space-saving designs reduce transmission weight and improve fuel efficiency.

Chemical Processing

Pump Seals: Hastelloy C-22 springs resist oxidizing acids (nitric, sulfuric) and reducing acids (hydrochloric). Springs maintain seal face pressure in high-temperature process pumps.

Relief Valves: Monel 400 springs for ammonia and alkali service. Alloy 20 springs for sulfuric acid applications. Precise spring rates ensure accurate pressure relief settings.

Reactor Components: Exotic alloy springs (Tantalum, Zirconium) for highly corrosive chemical reactions. Springs designed for thermal cycling between ambient and process temperatures.
