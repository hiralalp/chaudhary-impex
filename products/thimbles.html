Load Capacity & Safety Factors

Safe Working Load (SWL): The safe working load for wire rope thimbles is calculated based on the breaking strength of the wire rope and the thimble design. A safety factor of 5:1 is typically applied for general lifting operations, while critical applications may require 8:1 or higher.

Design Factor: Thimbles are designed to support the full rated capacity of the wire rope when properly spliced. The thimble material and thickness must be adequate to prevent wire rope crushing or deformation under load.

Installation Guidelines

Proper Installation:

  • Size Selection: Match thimble to wire rope diameter exactly
  • Material Compatibility: Use stainless thimbles with stainless wire rope
  • Bend Radius: Ensure thimble provides adequate bend radius (min 4x rope diameter)
  • Rope Seating: Wire rope must seat fully in thimble groove
  • Termination: Secure with proper clips, splices, or swaged fittings

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

  • Using undersized thimbles (causes rope damage)
  • Mixing dissimilar metals (causes galvanic corrosion)
  • Improper clip installation (reduces rope strength)
  • Inadequate inspection schedule

Inspection & Maintenance

Regular Inspection Points:

  • Wear: Check for groove wear, sharp edges
  • Corrosion: Look for pitting, rust, discoloration
  • Deformation: Check for bending, crushing, cracks
  • Rope Condition: Inspect wire rope for broken wires, kinks
  • Terminations: Verify clips, splices, swages are secure

Replacement Criteria:

  • Groove wear exceeding 10% of original depth
  • Visible cracks or deformation
  • Significant corrosion or pitting
  • Any damage that affects rope seating
