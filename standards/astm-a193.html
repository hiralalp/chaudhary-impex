ASTM A193 Specification

Alloy Steel and Stainless Steel Bolting for High Temperature Service

ASTM A193 Overview

ASTM A193 covers alloy steel and stainless steel bolting materials for pressure vessels, valves, flanges, and fittings for high temperature or high-pressure service, or both. This specification is one of the most widely used standards for industrial bolting worldwide.

Grade Selection Guide

When to Use Each Grade:

  • B7: General high-temperature, high-pressure service up to 538Â°C. Most economical choice.
  • B7M: Larger sizes (over 2Â½") where B7 cannot meet mechanical properties throughout section.
  • B16: Higher temperature service up to 593Â°C. Better creep resistance than B7.
  • B8/B8M Class 1: Corrosive environments, high temperature. Easy disassembly required.
  • B8/B8M Class 2: Higher strength needed in corrosive environments. Limited temperature.
  • B8C/B8T: Temperatures 427-816Â°C to prevent sensitization. Stabilized grades.
